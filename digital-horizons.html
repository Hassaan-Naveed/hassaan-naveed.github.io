Overview

During the Digital Horizons week during teaching week 7, I went to a couple of events, totalling in approximately 6 hours of CPD hours. I attended the Google Assistant talk and the Google Asssistant technical challange.

Google Assistant Talk

I attended the Google Assistant talk, which was held on the Wednesday of digital horizons week. This was of two parts: a talk by a Google Assistant developer, and a workshop.

The talk was about how apps, also known as actions, are developed for the Google Assistant, and focused primarily on the rules and guidelines to keep in mind when developing one. For example, one was to always fully create a personality for the action and follow said personality, and always keep responses short.

After the talk, there was a workshop session where we had to follow a short, hour long course on creating our own simple Google Assistant action. In order to do this we used the Google Dev Tools, and two add-on programs; FireBase and DialogueFlow. The action asked the user for their favourite colour and responded with the number of characters in the input.

Google Assistant Technical Challenge

I attended the Google Assistant Technical Challange on the Thursday of digital horizons week. This was a competition to create an action on the Google Assistant console using DialogueFlow, of a specific topic. The brief was to make an app that provided information on support services available at NTU when the Assisstant was asked.

The session started with being placed in groups and being told the brief. Afterwards we were allowed to begin. We first began by planning the program out, defining each section of the brief as a goal. Then I started trying to create the program, while the rest of my group were helping by offering suggestions and performing research. I largely used the tutorial from the prior day but appliead it to the new scenario. We did not win the challenge due to facing an issue that took too long to solve, and therefore not having a complete program, but we came 2nd place in the room we were in.
