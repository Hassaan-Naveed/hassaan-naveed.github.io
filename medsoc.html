Overview

The Medieval society represents the Nottingham Historia Normannis group. They aim to perform live recreations of historical battles, using real steel armour and weaponry. There are two main branches of the group:

  • Crafts
  • Combat

The crafts strand aims to teach people skills such as woodworking, fletching, leatherworking, and blacksmithing, in order to create new gear to use during training and recreations. The Combat strand aims to teach people controlled medieval combat, in preparation for the battle recreatiuons. This occurs every sunday at the Arboretum from 11am until 4pm. I attend the combat sessions.


Combat

I attend the combate training sessions every Sunday at The Arboretum. I either do one-on-one sparring to train in a new weapon, or group training. New recruits must start on wooden weapons to learn safety and control. I enjoy using either a shield and a wooden knife, or a wooden spear.


Session 1

In the first session I went to. we were trained to control the amount of force we used. We were also taught the acceptable areas to strike people. These were below the neck, above the knees, and above the elbows, in order to minimise injury to people. We were then trained one-on-one using just a wooden shortsword, and then a shield as well. Once we were deemed ready, we were allowed into the "arena", where we did free-for-all and group training. This lasted for 5 hours.


Session 2

My second session was a couple of weeks later, as a session the next week was called off due to heavy rain. During this, I opted to train in a different weapon - the spear. After learning how to properly and safely control it, I was able to use it in the "arena". During the line fights I ended up performing well. This lasted another 5 hours.
